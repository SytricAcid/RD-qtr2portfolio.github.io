The second quarter of the school year felt like navigating a winding river—sometimes calm, other times rushing with intensity. Each task I encountered was getting rougher, adding complexity to this quarter, and every performance task and test felt like rapids, testing my resilience and determination. At times, the current was swift and overwhelming, especially when deadlines loomed like rocky obstacles in the water.

For instance, the stress of dealing with notes to be submitted, end of quarter outputs to be made, they're all stacking on top of each other so quickly. Yet, just as a river carves its path through stone, I found ways to adapt, to persist, and to push through the challenges.

There were also moments of stillness—like serene pools in the river—where I could reflect on what I had learned and how I had grown. These moments taught me that progress isn’t always about rushing forward but sometimes about pausing, reassessing, and finding clarity in the calm.

Of course, there were moments wherein it felt like exploring nature, exciting and full of adventures. Events in LPSCI, such as the 2024 Intramurals that shined the spotlight on our student athletes, AP Month Couture which riled up each section for a fashion show, the English Month Booklandia wherein students showcased famous book characters, and even the upcoming EsP Month V-POP have really made the experience this quarter wonderful and enjoyable.

As the quarter came to a close, I realized that the river of learning doesn’t have a final destination but flows onward, always moving, always changing. Though the second quarter was challenging, it shaped me, smoothing the rough edges of doubt and sharpening my skills, much like water shapes the stones it encounters And now, as I prepare for the next phase, I carry with me the lessons of perseverance and the understanding that growth, like a river, is constant and unstoppable. Water holds memories, and these memories will flow throughout my lifetime.
